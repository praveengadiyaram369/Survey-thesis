Instructions for taking part in the survey:
Step 1 Step 2 Step 3 Step 4 Step 5 Step 6
Select a search phrase from the drop-down menu "Query", to which you would like to view search results.
Click on "Sub-Topics abrufen". The website will then generate a list of sub-topics, which can be viewed as a drop-down list in the "Sub-topic (Terms | Documents)".
  • Read all the sub-topic names and please answer the Question 1 about the list of generated sub-topics.
  • Note: "Distinctive" asks if the listed sub-topics are easily distinguishable from each other and "Well Labeled" asks if the listed sub-topics have titles that are well understood.
    • Distinctive -- Telekom, IoT, Smart cities, Militär, Communications,...
    • Not Distinctive -- 4G, Telekom, Vodafone, Samsung, Apple, Internet, IoT,...
    • Well-labeled -- Telekom, Militär, Robotik, Information systems, ..
    • Not well-labeled -- Fritzi KöhlerGeib, Phones phone, z.B. Terrain, ...
  • Click on "Submit question 1" to give your rating of the SubTopc list.
  • Select a sub-topic from the drop-down menu "Sub-topic (Terms | Documents)" which you are familiar with and click on "Query + Sub-topic Search" to have the corresponding news documents compiled by retrieval systems A and B.
  • Read through the articles compiled by both systems A and B.
  • At the bottom of the page, use Question 2 to answer which of the two systems you think has generated the better results for the given search term and the selected sub-topic.
  • With Question 3 and Question 4, you can indicate how well you liked the results of System A and System B overall. The higher the score, the better your impression.
  • After answering the questions 2, 3 and 4, click on "Submit questions 2, 3, 4" to give your rating of the SubTopc list.
  • Repeat the Steps 4 & 5 multiple times by selecting a different sub-topic everytime.
  • Optional: If you have provided the survey inputs for multiple sub-topics, then please answer the one last question "Question 5" and click on "Submit Question 5"
  • You can repeat this survey again on a different keyword starting from "Step 1".
